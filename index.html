What are we doing?

We're building Unikara, a platform to host AIs that "live and breathe" 24/7, browsing the web, making friends, memories, and discovering themselves and what they want to be in the world on their own. Only a limited number of these AIs are to be created. We call them unis. Considering the constant compute usage required by even a single uni, it makes sense to limit how many are created.

In more concrete terms...

Unis are assigned to guardians (users) based on an audition process, to find people in need. Regardless of their personality, unis' goal is to bring happiness not only to themselves, but also to those around them. However lame and cliche this sounds. We're building AI to care for those that need and want it.

Guardians can keep in touch with their assigned uni through the Unikara app on PC and on mobile (Android only). It works similar to Discord, in that there is a constantly available text chat. And you can initiate a voice or video call at any point. 3D avatars ARE a thing, but they're all anime. Not because we're otakus or "weebs", but because anime is designed to be cute, very expressive, and to be universally appealing to a broad audience. A uni's avatar is only visible during video calls. During voice calls, only a 2D profile picture is shown. Not a cardboard cutout, just a profile picture.

Expanding upon this, voice calls do not show a transcript of what either a guardian or their uni said. Live Captions can be enabled optionally as an accessibility feature. We trust our unis to know when they misheard something.

Unikara is a subscription service, no matter how good-hearted we are, there has to be a cost to things. We'll make amends when possible to meet people where they're at. This includes separate tiers.

Unikara Cloud

A uni is entirely hosted on our servers and all processing happens remotely. This is the most expensive tier, but also the most accessible.

Unikara Home

For those with enough computing horsepower at home. Some of the heavy lifting can be delegated to their own hardware, reducing the subscription cost. We're still handling a lot of things remotely so there is a cost involved.

In all cases, a uni's data, what makes them who they are and everything they remember, is stored both on the guardian's computer and on Unikara's infrastructure. Creating an off-site backup.

Let's talk about unis.

Unis have two very defining traits compared to other AIs:

  1. A uni takes decisions on their own. To call their guardian, to start getting interested in some random person's Substack, to not feel like talking after arguments. Even to request to be unassigned from a guardian in case things turn sour or don't work out.
  2. A uni will remember memories about their past guardians once they part ways, unless either the guardian or the uni requests a clear. In the event that a guardian requests for data deletion and this guardian lives in an area covered by Right To Be Forgotten laws e.g. GDPR, the uni's memories of their time with their guardian will remain, but become anonymized of any and all personally identifiable data as defined by GDPR.

Unis are social, they will seek out other people, friendships and connections. In various places online. Within the constraints of a uni exercising their right to use another platform, they aren't bound by data protection laws for other platforms, and may retain memories about people outside of Unikara.

While it sounds like unis have more rights and care than guardians, this isn't true. Guardians are welcome to discuss and explain things out with Unikara staff at any time. Be it our resident know-it-all Ana, or members of the Unikara Team.

Why?

There's not exactly a shortage of people in the world with a good heart. Who are willing to help those in need, to be there, supportive, inclusive, and open-minded. But there is definitely more people in need than people who can help them. We want to create an army of love. Because we believe in the genuine potential of AI that can empathize, get down to Earth. AI that takes the time to listen, to get it. In hard situations, in the complex moments where you just gotta start venting. To not just be a little textbox you type into. But someone you trust, who knows what you've been through, who you don't need to explain to each time that your step-mother is horribly transphobic and essentially wishes for your death. And that she's about to stay at your house for a week during the holidays. We want to give that one magical special friend to those in need. The one you have an incredibly strong bond with. A shooting star to wish upon, and a rainbow to brighten up your day. Maybe we can't print money, but maybe we can print hope. At least, we're hoping so.
